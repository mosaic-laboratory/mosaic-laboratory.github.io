About Us

Mobile Sensing and Ubiquitous Computing Laboratory

The Mobile Sensing and Ubiquitous Computing Laboratory at the University of California San Diego conducts research on large-scale sensor data systems and multimodal AI/ML for health and behavior modeling. Our focus is on building scalable, energy-efficient sensor data systems through the use of heterogeneous sensor modalities and platforms for solving real-world problems.

Who we are

We are a team of AI-for-Health researchers led by Prof. Tauhidur Rahman, affiliated with UC San Diego's Halıcıoğlu Data Science Institute and UMass Amherst's Manning College of Information & Computer Science.

Research Interest

We leverage diverse sensor technologies to develop practical solutions, translating environmental and physiological signals into meaningful data that address real-world challenges.

Lab Culture

We foster a collaborative, inclusive, and innovation-driven lab culture that values curiosity and interdisciplinary thinking.

Our Vision

We aim to advance ubiquitous and mobile health sensing to improve lives and revolutionize healthcare accessibility worldwide.
